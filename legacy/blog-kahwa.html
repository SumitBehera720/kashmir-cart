The Art of Kahwa: A Guide to Kashmir's Traditional Elixir

Published by Kashmir Heritage Editorial • May 15, 2026

For centuries, entering a home in Kashmir meant being welcomed by the steaming warmth of a copper samovar. The drink inside? Kahwa—a golden, spiced tea that is much more than a simple beverage. It is a symbol of welcoming hospitality and a centuries-old wellness remedy.

1. The Alchemy of Spices

Unlike traditional black tea, Kahwa is prepared using light hand-rolled green tea leaves. The magic lies in the ingredients steeped alongside the leaves. Green cardamoms, cinnamon bark, rose petals, and real Pampore saffron threads boil together to create a warm, aromatic water. To finish the drink, finely sliced sweet Mamra almonds are placed at the bottom of the cup, adding a rich, crunchy texture.

2. Health in Every Sip

Historically served to visiting kings and merchants traveling along the Silk Road, Kahwa is highly valued for its health benefits. It boosts metabolic rate, works as a natural digestive after rich meals, and serves as an instant cure for colds and sore throats. The combination of cardamoms and rose petals helps soothe digestion, while saffron releases active antioxidants that support mental focus and stress reduction.
