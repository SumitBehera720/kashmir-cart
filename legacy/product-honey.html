Heritage Story

Himalayan Bee Foraging

High in the pine-scented valleys of Kashmir, wild honeybees construct massive hives on the branches of ancient Lote (Sidr) trees. These trees bloom for only a brief period each year, producing tiny, highly aromatic yellow flowers.

Our beekeepers climb the rugged woodland trees at midnight, using traditional smoke-repelling methods to collect the honeycomb. No high-heat systems are used during extraction, ensuring that the honey retains its rich bio-active profile, natural pollen grains, and royal nutrients. It is the closest thing to eating straight from the hive.

About Wild Sidr Honey
Our Kashmiri Sidr Honey is extracted using zero-pressure gravity filtration. This means we do not heat the comb (which kills healthy enzymes) nor do we run it through micro-mesh filters that strip out the highly beneficial floral pollen. Every jar contains pure, wild nectar containing natural medicinal properties.
