Heritage Story

The Steaming Samovar Ritual

In Kashmir, hospitality is not a simple gesture—it is an art form. At the heart of this art is the traditional copper "Samovar". Handcrafted by Srinagar metalworkers, these vessels are fueled by burning coal in a central pipe, keeping the spiced water boiling at a perfect temperature.

Our Royal Shahi Kahwa replicates this exact palace experience. It is not just tea; it is a complex infusion of native mountain herbs and dry fruits that warm the body from within. Historically served to visiting emperors and silk road traders, it is a drink of welcome, hospitality, and luxury.

About This Luxury Tea Blend
Our Royal Kahwa is built with high-grown, unbroken green tea leaves from the Himalayan foothills. We mix these leaves with freshly crushed cardamoms from local spice traders, real cinnamon bark, dried rose petals, and real Pampore saffron threads. Every tin contains a packet of pre-sliced sweet Mamra almonds to add that traditional crunch.

How to Brew (Traditional Method)
1. Bring 2 cups of water to a rolling boil in a pot.
2. Add 1 teaspoon of Kashmir Heritage Shahi Kahwa loose tea along with a small piece of crushed ginger (optional) and 2 teaspoons of sugar or honey.
3. Simmer gently for 2-3 minutes until the water takes on a brilliant amber/golden shade.
4. Turn off the heat and strain the golden tea into your cups. Top it off with a pinch of the sliced almonds provided. Enjoy hot.
