Heritage Story

The Legend Behind the Red Gold

For more than two thousand years, the Kashmiri highlands have given birth to Saffron, famously known in local folklore as "Kumkum" or "Shung". Cultivated in the historic Karewa beds of Pampore, this spice is fed by mineral-rich glacial waters and sun-baked soils.

Every single strand is composed of the hand-detached crimson stigma of the purple saffron crocus flower. It takes over 150,000 blossoms, picked by hand in the chilly autumn dawns, to yield just one kilogram of dry Mongra threads. This complex artisan dedication is what turns our saffron into the most prized culinary and cosmetic gold in the world.

About This Luxury Saffron
Our Kashmiri Mongra Saffron consists purely of the top crimson stigmas of the Crocus sativus flower, containing zero yellow style threads (which are commonly left in lower-grade Spanish or Iranian saffron to add weight). It is harvested in October and November and sun-dried in shadows to preserve the delicate medicinal oils.

How to Use (Traditional Recipes)
The Infusion Method (Crucial): Never toss dry saffron threads straight into food. Steep 4-6 threads in a small cup of warm milk or water (approx. 50°C) for 15-20 minutes. The liquid will turn a brilliant golden yellow. Pour this rich golden infusion into your Biryani, Kheer, Risotto, or Kahwa tea during the final minutes of cooking.
Frequently Asked Questions

Is your saffron ISO certified?

Yes. Every batch is certified under ISO 3632 standards, proving our coloring strength exceeds Grade 1 requirements.

Why does it taste bitter?

Authentic saffron must taste bitter because of Picrocrocin. If saffron tastes sweet on your tongue, it has been treated with sugar water or syrup and is counterfeit.
